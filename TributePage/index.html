Marie Curie

The first woman to be awarded a Nobel Prize

A timeline of Madame Curie's life:

  • 1867 - Marie Curie is born Marie Sklodowska in Warsaw, Poland on November 7.
  • 1891 - She moves to France to study at the University of Paris, also known as Sorbonne.
  • 1895 - Marries Pierre Curie in Sceaux, a town in Paris. She in known comtemporarily as "Pierre's biggest discovery."
  • 1897 - She gives birth to her first daughter, Irene.
  • 1898 - While working on routine experiments she discovers the elements of polonium & radium. Polonium was named for her native Poland.
  • 1898-1902 - Throughout these years, she & Pierre publish 32 different papers including one that shows the usage of radium to destroy diseased, tumor-forming cells in the body.
  • 1903 - She & her husband win the Nobel Prize in Physics for their research in radiation.
  • 1905 - After Pierre's death in a road accident, she assumes his role at the University of Paris, becoming the first woman to teach there.
  • 1911 - She is awarded another Nobel Prize, this time in Chemistry, for her continued efforts in advancing radioactivity.
  • 1914 - She assists in the war through her radioactive research, using her form of "X-rays" to identify soldier's wounds & establishing France's first military radiology center.
  • 1922 - Curie becomes a member of the French Academy of Medicine, which under her leadership would produce four other Nobel Prize winners.
  • 1929 - She and her sister open a research institute in her native Warsaw, which still bears her name today.
  • 1934 - After a lifelong career devoted to advancing radioactivity in modern medicine, its negative effects take their toll and she passes away.

    • "Nothing in life is to be feared, it is only to be understood. Now is the time to understand more, so that we may fear less."

    -- Madame Curie
